# C.P.E. Bach: Andante Generation Rules

*Versuch über die wahre Art das Clavier zu spielen* (1753/1762)

---

## Quick Reference Tables

### Appoggiatura Application

| Context | Duration | Notes |
|---------|----------|-------|
| Duple time | Half of principal note | Downbeat or upbeat |
| Triple time | Two-thirds of principal | Downbeat only |
| Before cadence | Long (variable) | With fermata |
| Before trill | Short or omitted | Depends on tempo |
| Filling thirds | Short (schnell) | Ascending melodic |
| Affettuoso | Profuse | Releases expire pp |

**Execution**: Louder than resolution; slurred; held until release.

### Trill Application

| Type | Context | Execution |
|------|---------|-----------|
| Standard | Long notes, cadences | From upper note |
| Short (Prall) | Quick notes | Brief snap |
| With suffix | Cadential | Add Nachschlag |
| Ascending | After stepwise rise | Upper auxiliary |
| Descending | After stepwise fall | Begins on note |

**Rules**: Practice slowly first; never faster than even; suffix as fast as trill.

### Turn Application

| Position | Context | Notes |
|----------|---------|-------|
| Over note | Sustained notes | Standard form |
| After note | Before rest/tie | Anticipatory |
| Over dot | Dotted rhythms | Fills duration |
| Trilled turn | Cadential emphasis | Combined ornament |
| Snapped turn | Quick passages | Brief execution |

**Avoid**: Rapid descending passages; congested textures.

### Mordent Application

| Context | Use |
|---------|-----|
| Short notes | Standard application |
| Detached passages | Adds articulation |
| Bass notes | Especially appropriate |
| Long notes | Use trill instead |

### Slow Movement (Adagio) Expression

| Element | Principle |
|---------|-----------|
| Touch | Broad, sustained, never sticky |
| Ornaments | Sustain tone; conceal keyboard decay |
| Dynamics | Express affect; soften resolutions |
| Tempo | Avoid dragging; don't rush |
| Character | Singing (cantabile); from the soul |
| Space | Fill empty gaps without excess |

## Trill Rules

- **§4**: The performer has no need to be unduly concerned about the proper location of the trill, for its acknowledged symbols are almost always notated.

- **§6**: However, since the long mordent has almost the same symbol, I think it better to retain the m and avoid confusion.

- **§7**: They must be practiced industriously from the start.

- **§8**: Trill slowly at first and then more rapidly but always evenly.

- **§9**: The trill must be practiced diligently with all fingers.

- **§14**: However, instead of the usual extremely rapid motion into the following note, when dotted notes are trilled a very short separation must be made between the last tone of the suffix and the following note.

- **§15**: The suffix must be played as rapidly as the trill proper.

- **§20**: No matter how enticing the appearance of such notes, they must not be trilled.

- **§23**: The ascending trill requires a long note, for it comprises many tones, including the normal suffix.

- **§25**: Over long notes of several bars, trills that threaten to lapse can be revived by means of the ascending prefix, but it must be interpolated without causing the slightest discontinuity.

## Turn Rules

- **§64**: Crossing and turning, the principal means of changing the fingers, must be applied in such a manner that the tones involved in the change flow smoothly.

- **§7**: Hence its correct use must be carefully investigated, for, despite its complaisance, many apparent opportunities arise which are not actually suitable to it.

- **§8**: Hence, passages which must be played undecorated and sustained because of the affect are ruined by those who insert a turn because of the length of the notes, in ignorance of style and touch.

- **§26**: The required snap in the turn, at which the little finger is not adept, requires an occasional slightly forced fingering.

- **§36**: The snapped turn should not be confused with the simple turn after a note.

## Mordent Rules

- **§4**: It seldom appears in descending leaps and never in descending seconds.

- **§8**: When the tempo is so slow that even a long mordent will not fill out the notes adequately, they may be shortened.

- **§12**: Occasionally an unusual fingering must be taken in order to keep the best fingers free and ready to perform a mordent preceded by a short note.

- **§15**: While discussing mordents, I must make mention of an arbitrary decoration often performed by singers in slow movements at the beginning and before fermate and rests.

## Slide Rules

- **§4**: The two-toned slide is distinguished from the three-toned in that it is always used in a leap which it helps to fill in.

- **§2**: The affect with which the slide is sometimes performed requires that the initial tone be held beyond its usual duration, with the result that the entrance of the succeeding chord must be delayed.

- **§3**: But certain executions of the ornament require slight changes in the accompaniment.

---

## Snap Rules

- **§2**: The snap is always played rapidly and appears only before quick, detached notes, to which it imparts brilliance while serving to fill them out.

## Cadence Rules

- **§36**: In a final cadence the fifth must never appear in the upper voice.

- **§8**: But formal closing cadences are not always required; they are employed at the end and once in the middle.

- **§9**: When, in a recitative with accompanying instruments, the bass enters ahead of the other performers after a cadence or pause, the keyboardist must strike his chord and bass note strictly on time.
