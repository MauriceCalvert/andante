# Koch's Mechanical Rules of Melody - Condensed

**Source**: Heinrich Christoph Koch, *Versuch einer Anleitung zur Composition* (1782-1793)
**Purpose**: Core rules for melodic composition, applicable to andante/slow movement generation

---

## Quick Reference: Key Constraints

### Phrase Types
- **Basic phrase** (enger Satz): Minimum content to be understood as complete section
- **Extended phrase** (erweiterter Satz): Basic phrase + clarification/definition
- **Compound phrase** (zusammengeschobener Satz): Two+ phrases combined as one
- **I-phrase** (Grundabsatz): Caesura on tonic triad
- **V-phrase** (Quintabsatz): Caesura on dominant triad
- **Closing phrase** (Schlußsatz): Contains formal cadence

### Phrase Lengths
- **4 measures**: Most common, most pleasing (Vierer)
- **5 measures**: Extended from 4, OR unequal segment connection (Fünfer)
- **6 measures**: Extended from 4 by 2 measures (Sechser)
- **7 measures**: Rare, from extending 5-measure phrases

### Incises (Phrase Segments)
- **Complete incise**: 2 measures, can stand somewhat alone
- **Incomplete incise**: 1 measure, must be followed by completion
- Incomplete incises usually appear in pairs (balanced)
- Caesuras of incises can use 6th chords; phrase caesuras cannot

### Phrase Sequence Rules (CRITICAL)
| First | Second | Allowed? | Notes |
|-------|--------|----------|-------|
| I-phrase | V-phrase | YES | Most common |
| I-phrase | I-phrase | NO | Unpleasant effect |
| V-phrase | I-phrase | NO | Not at composition start |
| V-phrase | V-phrase | NO in same key | YES in different keys |
| V-phrase | Cadence | YES | Closes period |
| I-phrase | Cadence | YES | If first cadence in main key |

### Caesura Rules
- Caesura notes MUST fall on strong beat of measure
- Decorated caesuras: (1) subsidiary notes, (2) appoggiatura/suspension, (3) filled space to next phrase
- Fermatas allowed at phrase caesuras (temporarily suspends meter)
- Root of caesura chord in bass (except incises may use 6th chord)

### Extension Methods
1. **Repetition**: Most frequent; repeat phrase or segment (varied or exact)
   - Single measure: same key only
   - Complete incise (2 measures): any key
   - If incomplete incises repeated, must repeat BOTH
   
2. **Appendix** (Anhang): Add clarifying segment after phrase-ending
   - Strengthens content without changing rhythmic value
   - Can close on different harmony than main phrase
   
3. **Sequence** (Progression): Repeat segment on different scale degrees
   - Minimum 2 measures per segment
   - MUST maintain segment equality throughout
   - Breaking segment equality = unpleasant effect
   
4. **Parenthesis**: Insert incidental material between phrase segments
   - After incomplete incise: insert 1 measure
   - After complete incise: insert 2+ measures
   - Preserve segment balance

### Period Structure (16-measure standard)
```
First Period (8 bars):
  Phrase 1: I-phrase or V-phrase (4 bars)
  Phrase 2: Cadence in I or V (4 bars)

Second Period (8 bars):
  Phrase 3: Modulation/development (4 bars)  
  Phrase 4: Cadence in I (4 bars)
```

### Modulation Rules
- **Major key first period**: Cadence in V (dominant)
- **Minor key first period**: Cadence in v (minor dominant) OR III (relative major)
- Second period returns to tonic via passing modulations
- Related keys only: V, vi, IV, ii for major; III, v, iv, VII for minor

### Metrical Stress
- Accompanying voices MUST maintain metrical motion when melody rests
- Tied notes: accompaniment fills the beat
- Maximum 2 consecutive tied subdivisions before accompaniment required
- Violations of metrical stress = "unpleasant effect"

## Detailed Rules by Topic

### 1. Definitions (§77-85)

**§77**: Resting points divide melody into comprehensible sections, analogous to punctuation in speech. Without them, melody cannot affect feelings properly.

**§78**: Melodic sections distinguished by: (1) type of ending (punctuation), (2) length and proportion.

**§80**: Three phrase types by completeness:
- Basic phrase: minimum for independent section
- Extended phrase: basic + clarification  
- Compound phrase: multiple phrases appearing as one

**§82**: Basic phrase complete when felt as self-sufficient section, even if requiring additional material to complete the period.

**§83**: Phrases either: (1) cohere closely without internal resting points, or (2) contain caesuras dividing them into incises.

**§85**: Basic phrases may contain more than one incise (more than two incomplete segments).

### 2. Phrase Length (§86-95)

**§86**: Length depends on whether basic or extended. This section covers basic phrases only.

**§87**: Four-measure phrases most common, useful, and pleasing. May appear as 4 simple measures or 2 compound measures.

**§89**: Phrases of 5-6-7 measures exist but should not be confused with extended 4-measure phrases. Different treatment required for joining.

**§90**: Six-measure phrases arise: (1) extending 4-measure unit, (2) connecting 3+3 incomplete segments, (3) connecting segments of unequal length.

**§92**: In compound meter, each measure = 2 simple measures. Phrases in simple meter may be inserted into compound meter contexts.

**§93**: Caesura = place where one section separates from following. Caesuras should properly always fall on strong beat.

**§94**: Cadence endings fundamentally different from phrase endings (cadences close wholes, phrases cannot). Incise endings similar to phrase endings.

**§95**: Caesura notes usually decorated with subsidiary notes. Various decorations create different ending formulas.

### 3. Caesura and Endings (§96-106)

**§96**: Decoration by suspension/appoggiatura: non-chord tone on downbeat, caesura note follows.

**§97**: Decoration by filling: space from caesura to next phrase filled with notes, connecting phrases more closely.

**§98**: Incise caesuras follow phrase caesura rules. However, incises may use 6th chord bass (phrase caesuras cannot).

**§99**: Fermata may briefly interrupt metrical movement at phrase caesura. Can appear on caesura note or as overhang on following downbeat.

**§100**: I-phrase = caesura on tonic triad. V-phrase = caesura on dominant triad. V-phrase with fermata often called "half cadence."

**§101**: Phrase caesura root ALWAYS in bass. Exceptions exist with 6th chord caesura in incises only.

**§102**: Cadence formula has three notes: (1) preparation note on strong beat, (2) cadential note proper, (3) caesura note. Preparation decorated with passing/neighbor tones.

**§103**: Cadence formula may be augmented (each note = full measure) or diminished. Augmented preparation often decorated.

**§104**: Cadence caesura may have appendix strengthening the close (repeated tonic triads in full scoring).

**§105-106**: Phrase extension through repetition. Single measure repetition: same key only. Multi-measure repetition: different keys possible.

### 4. Period Structure (§17-45)

**§17**: Learn connection of sections through shortest compositions: dance melodies, songs, short instrumental pieces.

**§18**: Gavotte characteristics: 2/2 meter, settled tempo, begins on upbeat with 2 quarters, even-numbered rhythmic sections, incise in 2nd measure.

**§21**: Dance melodies and small pieces use basic 4-measure phrases. These are most common, useful, and pleasing.

**§22**: Shortest compositions usually 16 measures (4 four-measure phrases).

**§23**: With 4 sections, either: (1) only last contains cadence, or (2) two sections have cadences. If two cadences, either both in main key OR first in related key.

**§24**: Two cadences usually in phrases 2 and 4, dividing whole into two periods. May be performed with or without repetition.

**§25**: When phrase 2 cadences in main key: phrase 1 can be V-phrase, phrase 3 can be I-phrase.

**§29**: First cadence in main key divides composition into two equal periods.

**§30**: When cadencing in subsidiary key (usually V in major, v or III in minor), first cadence made by phrase 2.

**§31**: Major mode, phrase 2 cadences in V: four punctuation forms possible for phrases 1 and 3.

**§34**: Phrase-ending sequences limited by harmonic basis. Neither two I-phrases nor two V-phrases may follow directly in same key with different melodic content.

**§35**: Two I-phrases in succession: second almost always produces unpleasant effect.

**§36**: Two V-phrases prohibited in same key. Allowed if: (1) repetition of same phrase, or (2) different keys.

**§37**: Opening V-phrase should NOT be followed by I-phrase. Must be followed by cadence OR modulation to new V-phrase.

### 5. Extension Methods (§46-72)

**§46**: Extension methods already described in Vol. 2, Section 3. Now learn their application.

**§48**: Extension through repetition: entire phrases or segments. Occurs on same harmony OR different harmony.

**§49**: Phrase without incomplete incises: any measure (1st, 2nd, 3rd) can be repeated. Creates uneven 5-measure phrase.

**§50**: Phrase with incises: matters whether complete or incomplete. Incomplete incises: if first repeated, second MUST be repeated too.

**§51**: Complete incises (2 measures): usually entire segment repeated.

**§52**: Repetition of 2-measure incise or whole phrase more common than single-measure repetition.

**§55**: Appendix to I-phrase or V-phrase: usually repetition of second segment. May be exact or varied.

**§64**: Displacement/sequence: repeat segment on other scale degrees. Single repetition: minimum 2 measures.

**§66**: CRITICAL: Sequence must maintain segment equality until phrase end. Breaking equality = unpleasant effect.

**§69**: Passage (passaggio): continue a figure contained in phrase. Segment equality must be preserved.

**§70**: Parenthesis: interpolate incidental material between segments. After incomplete incise: insert uneven segment. After complete incise: insert even segment.

### 6. Meter and Rhythm (§1-16)

**§9**: Musical feet correspond to metrical divisions: trochaic, iambic, spondee, pyrrhic, etc.

**§10**: Once meter established, feeling accepts it. Immediate offense when movement interrupted in course.

**§13**: Metrical stress violations often due to inadequate accompanying voice movement, not just main melody.

**§14**: Tied subdivisions: meter depends on which division level. Two consecutive ties maximum before accompaniment required.

**§15**: Tones maintaining metrical stress may be multiplied (divided, mixed with passing notes) without offense.

**§16**: Exceptions exist between successive complete phrases where metrical stress temporarily absent.

## Application Notes for Andante Generation

1. **Default to 4-measure phrases** - Most natural length
2. **Respect phrase sequence rules** - I→V allowed, I→I forbidden
3. **Caesuras on strong beats** - Non-negotiable
4. **Maintain accompaniment during melody rests** - Metrical continuity
5. **Use extension sparingly** - Repetition most frequent, sequence requires segment balance
6. **Period structure: 8+8 bars** - First period establishes, second develops and returns
7. **Modulate appropriately** - Major→V, Minor→v or III for first period cadence
