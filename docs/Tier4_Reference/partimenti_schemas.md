# Partimenti.org: Galant Schemas

**Source:** https://partimenti.org/schemas/collections/galant/schema_prototypes.pdf  
**Author:** Robert Gjerdingen, *Music in the Galant Style* (2007)

---

## Schema Notation

- **①②③...** = Scale degrees in bass
- **❶❷❸...** = Scale degrees in melody
- **5/3, 6/3** = Figured bass (intervals above bass)
- **Strong/Weak** = Metric position

---

## Opening Gambits

### Romanesca

**Function:** Opening gambit  
**Period:** 1720s-1730s peak, available throughout century

| Event | Metric | Melody | Bass | Sonority |
|-------|--------|--------|------|----------|
| 1 | Strong | ❶ or ❺ | ① | 5/3 (major) |
| 2 | Weak | variable | ⑦ | 6/3 (major) |
| 3 | Strong | variable | ⑥ | 6/3 (minor) |
| 4 | Weak | ❶ or ❺ | ③ | 6/3 (major) |

**Variants:**
- Leaping type: bass leaps down 4th, up 2nd (all 5/3)
- Stepwise type: bass descends by step (alternating 5/3, 6/3)

---

### Do-Re-Mi

**Function:** Opening gambit  
**Period:** All decades, all genres

| Event | Metric | Melody | Bass | Sonority |
|-------|--------|--------|------|----------|
| 1 | Strong | ❶ | ① | 5/3 |
| 2 | Weak | ❷ | ⑦ (or ⑤) | 6/3 |
| 3 | Strong | ❸ | ① | 5/3 |

**Features:**
- Easily inverted (melody in bass, bass in melody)
- Favourite for imitative openings

**Variant:** Adeste Fidelis type with leaps to/from ❺

---

## Ripostes (Answers)

### Prinner

**Function:** Riposte to opening gambit  
**Period:** 1720s-1770s peak

| Event | Metric | Melody | Bass | Sonority |
|-------|--------|--------|------|----------|
| 1 | Strong | ❻ | ④ | 5/3 |
| 2 | Weak | ❺ | ③ | 6/3 |
| 3 | Strong | ❹ | ② | 6/3 (often dissonant) |
| 4 | Weak | ❸ | ① | 5/3 |

**Cadential variant:** Insert ❷ before final ❸, insert ⑤ before final ①

**Canon variant:** ❻-❺-❹-❸ in both melody and bass with pedal on ①

---

## Sequences

### Fonte

**Function:** Digression and return to main key  
**Period:** Throughout century, common after double bar

| Half | Mode | Event | Melody | Bass | Sonority |
|------|------|-------|--------|------|----------|
| 1 | Minor | 1 | ❻-❺-❹-❸ | ⑦ | 6/3 or 6/5/3 |
| 1 | Minor | 2 | (cadence) | ① | 5/3 |
| 2 | Major (step lower) | 3 | ❻-❺-❹-❸ | ⑦ | 6/3 or 6/5/3 |
| 2 | Major | 4 | (cadence) | ① | 5/3 |

**Key:** First half minor, second half major one step lower

---

### Monte

**Function:** Ascending sequence  
**Period:** Throughout century

| Section | Metric | Melody | Bass | Sonority |
|---------|--------|--------|------|----------|
| 1 | Strong | descending | ⑦ (chromatic) | 6/5/3 |
| 1 | Weak | | ① | 5/3 |
| 2 | Strong | (step higher) | ⑦ | 6/5/3 |
| 2 | Weak | | ① | 5/3 |

**Features:**
- Each section one step higher
- Bass: chromatic ascents from leading tones to local tonics
- Highlights subdominant → dominant in later 18th century

**Variants:**
- Diatonic type: 6-5-6-5 pattern
- Principale type: all 5/3, bass up 4th down 3rd
- Romanesca type: up 5th down 4th with 4-3 suspensions

## Thematic Schemas

### Meyer

**Function:** Important themes  
**Period:** 1760s-1780s peak

| Event | Metric | Melody | Bass | Sonority |
|-------|--------|--------|------|----------|
| 1 | Strong | ❶ | ① | 5/3 (open) |
| 2 | Weak | ❼ | ② | 6/3 |
| 3 | Strong | ❹ | ⑦ (or ⑤) | 6/5/3 |
| 4 | Weak | ❸ | ① | 5/3 (closed) |

**Solfeggio:** Both ❶-❼ and ❹-❸ are fa-mi in major

**Related schemas:**
- **Jupiter:** ❶-❷-❹-❸
- **Pastorella:** ❸-❷-❹-❸
- **Aprile:** ❶-❼-❷-❶

---

### Sol-Fa-Mi

**Function:** Important themes, slow movements  
**Period:** 1750s-1790s

| Event | Metric | Melody | Bass | Sonority |
|-------|--------|--------|------|----------|
| 1 | Strong | ❺ | ① | 5/3 (open) |
| 2 | Weak | ❹ | ② | 5/3 (often minor/dim) |
| 3 | Strong | ❹ | ⑦ (or ⑤) | 6/5/3 |
| 4 | Weak | ❸ | ① | 5/3 (closed) |

**Character:** Less assertive than Do-Re-Mi, common in Adagios

---

### Fenaroli

**Function:** "Second theme" after modulation to dominant  
**Period:** Named for the master

| Event | Metric | Melody | Bass | Sonority |
|-------|--------|--------|------|----------|
| 1 | Variable | ❹ (or ❷) | ⑦ | 6/5/3 |
| 2 | Variable | ❸ (or ❸) | ① | 5/3 |
| 3 | Variable | ❼ (or ❼) | ② | 6/3 |
| 4 | Variable | ❶ (or ❶) | ③ | 6/3 |

**Bass pattern:** ⑦-①-②-③ (mi-fa-re-mi in solfeggio)

**Durante countermelody:** ❺-❹-❸-❶-❼-❺-❶-❸

**Features:**
- Usually repeated
- Ends on 6/3, lacks finality
- Processive rather than thematic

## Framing Schemas

### Quiescenza

**Function:** Quiescence after important cadence  
**Period:** 1760s-1790s

| Event | Metric | Melody | Bass | Sonority |
|-------|--------|--------|------|----------|
| 1 | Weak | ♭❼ | ① | ♭7/3 |
| 2 | Strong | ❻ | ① | 6/4 |
| 3 | Weak | ♮❼ | ① | ♮7/4/2 |
| 4 | Strong | ❶ | ① | 5/3 |

**Features:**
- Bass pedal on ①
- Usually played twice
- Solfeggio: fa-mi answered by mi-fa

---

### Ponte

**Function:** Bridge on dominant before return  
**Period:** After double bar in minuets

| Event | Metric | Melody | Bass | Sonority |
|-------|--------|--------|------|----------|
| 1+ | Various | ❺,❼,❷,❹ | ⑤ (pedal) | 5/3 or 7/5/3 |

**Features:**
- Extended dominant
- Rising contour
- Delays expected resolution

---

### Indugio

**Function:** Teasing delay before Converging cadence  
**Period:** 1760s-1790s, common in Vienna/Paris

| Event | Metric | Melody | Bass | Sonority |
|-------|--------|--------|------|----------|
| 1+ | Various | ❷,❹,❻ | ④ (repeated) | 6/5/3 |
| final | Strong | | ⑤ | 5/3 |

**Features:**
- Iterations on ④, often with #④ approach
- "Darkening" to minor
- Storm-and-stress syncopations

### Schema Selection Algorithm

```python
def select_schema(context: PhraseContext) -> Schema:
    """Select appropriate schema for context."""
    if context.is_opening:
        return choice([DO_RE_MI, ROMANESCA])
    if context.follows_opening:
        return PRINNER  # riposte
    if context.after_double_bar:
        return choice([FONTE, PONTE])
    if context.needs_sequence:
        if context.direction == ASCENDING:
            return MONTE
        else:
            return FONTE
    if context.post_cadence:
        return QUIESCENZA
    if context.dominant_key:
        return FENAROLI
    if context.slow_tempo:
        return SOL_FA_MI
    return MEYER  # default thematic
```
