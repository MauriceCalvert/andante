Backward limit is roughly **1450** — Ockeghem, Josquin's generation. Before that, the counterpoint rules change fundamentally (medieval parallel fifths are correct, not forbidden; rhythm is mensural in ways that don't map onto bar lines). Your pipeline assumes barred metre and Bach-era consonance rules. Rewriting those assumptions is a different system, not a configuration change.

Forward limit is roughly **1780** — late Haydn, early Mozart. Once you hit Classical style proper, the music is driven by phrase-period structure (antecedent-consequent), homophonic texture with Alberti bass, and dramatic contrast between themes. Your system generates contrapuntal texture where every voice has melodic independence. Classical music has servants and masters — an accompaniment figure that exists only to support the melody. That's a fundamentally different generation model.

Within that 330-year window, some eras are closer than others:

**Easy reach** (style weights + form templates): Corelli trio sonatas, Purcell fantasias, Telemann canons, Vivaldi concerto ritornelli, Handel fugues. All orthodox Baroque counterpoint with genre variation.

**Moderate reach** (new mode system + relaxed dissonance rules): Gibbons, Byrd, Sweelinck, Frescobaldi. Renaissance-to-early-Baroque polyphony. Modal rather than tonal, but the counterpoint engine is the right shape.

**Hard but feasible** (significant new infrastructure): Palestrina — strict *stile antico* with flow rules (no repeated notes, specific dissonance preparation, semibreve pulse). The rules are more restrictive than Bach, not less, which paradoxically makes them easier to encode. But the rhythmic system is completely different (tactus, not barred metre). You'd need a new L1/metric layer.

**At the boundary**: C.P.E. Bach *Empfindsamer Stil* — still contrapuntal enough to work, but the expressive vocabulary (sudden dynamic shifts, rhetorical pauses, harmonic surprise) needs a tension model the system doesn't have. Possible but stretching.

**Beyond reach without a redesign**: anything after Haydn Op. 33 quartets, anything before Dufay. And anything primarily homophonic — Schubert songs, Mozart piano sonatas, opera — because the generation model is fundamentally polyphonic.

Your sweet spot is 1580–1750. That's a huge repertoire and nobody else is generating it.
